## What is Deep Water Culture (DWC)?

Deep Water Culture is a hydroponic method where plant roots are suspended directly in nutrient-rich, oxygenated water. **It's the simplest active hydroponic system** and delivers incredible growth rates.

Unlike soil gardening, DWC gives roots constant access to nutrients, water, and oxygen - resulting in **30-50% faster growth** than traditional methods.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** DWC is perfect for beginners because it's forgiving and requires minimal maintenance once set up properly.

## How DWC Works

The system is surprisingly simple:

- Plants sit in net pots filled with growing medium
- Roots dangle into nutrient solution below
- Air pump provides constant oxygen via air stones
- Plants absorb nutrients directly from water

**The key is oxygenation.** Without it, roots would drown. The air stones create tiny bubbles that oxygenate the water and deliver oxygen directly to the roots.

## Materials Needed

Here's everything you need for a basic 5-gallon DWC system:

**Container:**
- 5-gallon bucket with lid
- Must be opaque (blocks light to prevent algae)

**Growing Components:**
- 3-inch net pots
- Clay pebbles or hydroton (growing medium)
- Seeds or seedlings

**Aeration:**
- Air pump (rated for aquarium size)
- Air stone
- Air tubing

**Nutrients & Monitoring:**
- Hydroponic nutrient solution
- pH test kit or pH meter
- pH up/down solutions

> ⚠️ **Warning:** Never use garden fertilizer in hydroponics! You need nutrients specifically formulated for hydroponic systems.

## Step-by-Step Build Guide

### Step 1: Prepare the Container

**Drill holes** in the bucket lid for net pots. Use a 3-inch hole saw for perfect circles.

Space holes 6-8 inches apart to prevent plants from crowding.

> ✅ **Success:** If your net pots fit snugly in the holes without falling through, you're good to go!

### Step 2: Install Air Stone

Place the air stone at the bottom center of the bucket.

Run air tubing through a small hole drilled in the lid or leave a gap.

> ⚡ **Quick Tip:** Use two air stones for better oxygenation in larger buckets!

### Step 3: Fill with Nutrient Solution

Mix your nutrient solution according to package directions.

Fill bucket leaving 1-2 inches of space at top.

**Check pH:** Should be between 5.5-6.5 for most crops.

### Step 4: Plant Your Crops

Fill net pots with clay pebbles.

Place seedlings or seeds in the center.

Ensure bottom of net pot touches water (roots will grow down).

### Step 5: Turn On the Pump

Connect air pump to tubing and turn it on.

You should see steady stream of bubbles.

## What to Grow in DWC

**Best crops for beginners:**
- Lettuce (harvest in 4-6 weeks)
- Basil (ready in 3-4 weeks)
- Spinach (harvest in 5-7 weeks)
- Mint (grows like crazy)
- Cilantro (fast and forgiving)

**Advanced crops:**
- Tomatoes (needs larger container)
- Peppers (slow but rewarding)
- Cucumbers (requires support)

## Maintenance Schedule

**Daily:**
- Check water level (top off as needed)
- Verify air pump is running
- Look for signs of problems

**Weekly:**
- Test and adjust pH
- Check nutrient strength (EC/PPM)
- Inspect roots for health

**Bi-weekly:**
- Complete water change
- Clean bucket and equipment
- Refresh nutrients

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Keep a grow journal! Track pH, water changes, and plant progress. You'll learn what works for your setup.

## Common Problems & Solutions

**Problem: Roots turning brown or slimy**

**Solution:** This is root rot from insufficient oxygen or high temps. Lower water temperature and increase aeration.

**Problem: Slow growth**

**Solution:** Check pH first (most common issue). Then verify nutrients are correct strength.

**Problem: Algae in water**

**Solution:** Block all light from reaching water. Use opaque containers and cover any gaps in lid.
